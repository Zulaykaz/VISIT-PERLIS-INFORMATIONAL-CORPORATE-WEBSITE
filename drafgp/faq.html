Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the best time to visit Perlis?
The best time to visit Perlis is from November to February when the weather is cooler and rainfall is lower, making it ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities.
2. How can I travel around Perlis?
You can travel around Perlis by car, taxi, or bicycle. Public transport is limited, so renting a car or using ride-hailing services is recommended for convenience.
3. Are there any local customs I should know?
Visitors should respect local traditions, dress modestly when visiting religious sites, and always ask permission before taking photos of people.
4. Is Perlis safe for tourists?
Yes, Perlis is generally safe for tourists. As with any travel, keep your belongings secure and follow local guidelines.
5. What local dishes should I try?
You should try Perlis laksa, nasi dagang, and kuih-muih local desserts for an authentic culinary experience.
6. Where can I stay in Perlis?
Perlis offers a variety of accommodations, from budget hotels and guesthouses in Kangar to homestays near nature attractions like the Perlis State Park.
7. Are there any popular tourist attractions?
Popular attractions include Gua Kelam, Wang Kelian State Park, Perlis State Park, and Padang Besar for shopping.
8. What languages are commonly spoken?
Malay is the official language. English is widely understood in tourist areas, while some locals also speak Thai and dialects of Malay.
9. Can I use public transport to reach tourist sites?
Public transport is limited, so renting a car or using taxis or ride-hailing apps is recommended for visiting most attractions.
10. Are there any local festivals worth attending?
Yes, some popular festivals include the Perlis Harvest Festival, Chinese New Year celebrations, and Hari Raya Aidilfitri events showcasing local culture and cuisine.
